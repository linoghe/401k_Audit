Methodology

Data sources: Payroll deduction data was extracted from Gusto pay stub PDFs. Actual deposit data was extracted from Guideline's participant transaction list, which includes both "Requested date" and "Fulfilled date" for each transaction. Corroborating evidence was obtained from the 2025 W-2 (filed by the employer with the IRS) and Guideline's annual participant summaries.

Matching: Each payroll deduction was matched to the nearest Guideline payroll deposit within a {{ match_window_days }}-day window following the pay date. Guideline deposits combine employee contributions and employer match into a single transaction, so the expected deposit amount equals the employee deduction plus the employer match from the corresponding pay stub.

Unfunded contributions: Guideline transactions without a "Fulfilled date" indicate that the employer submitted the payroll contribution record but never transferred the actual funds. Under normal operations, payroll contributions are fulfilled within 3-6 business days. Transactions with no fulfilled date after weeks or months are treated as unfunded. This is independently confirmed by Guideline's annual summaries, which report these amounts as "Pending."

Late deposit threshold: The DOL requires employers to deposit withheld contributions as soon as they can reasonably be segregated from general assets, but no later than the 15th business day of the month following withholding. A threshold of {{ late_threshold_days }} calendar days was used.
