Matching Methodology

Each scheduled payment from Exhibit A was matched to the nearest actual paystub by pay date. A payment is classified as Late if the actual pay date is after the scheduled date, and Overdue if no corresponding payment has been received. Matching is sequential: the first post-separation payment fulfills the first scheduled obligation, the second fulfills the second, and so on. Amounts are compared at the gross level (${{ "{:,.2f}".format(audit.scheduled_payments[0].amount) }} per payment).

Legal Framework

Section 5 of the Agreement provides that "the Party claiming breach or non-compliance may file and pursue whatever civil action and/or actions in equity against the breaching or non-compliant Party" and that "the prevailing Party to any action initiated under the terms of this Agreement shall be reimbursed for costs and expenses, including reasonable attorney's fees." The Agreement is governed by Maryland law (Section 12).

Limitations

  • Healthcare continuation cannot be verified from paystub data alone; the employee should confirm coverage through insurance records.
  • This report covers gross payment compliance only. Net pay varies due to tax withholding and 401(k) deductions, which are separate from the severance obligation.
  • The Miro expense reimbursement status is inferred from the "Total Reimbursements" field on each paystub ($0.00 on all post-separation stubs). It is possible the expense was paid through a separate channel not reflected in payroll.
